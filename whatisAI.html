What Is AI




  • The concept of AI

    The Turing test (English: Turing test) is a thought experiment proposed by the British computer scientist Alan Turing in 1950. The purpose is to test whether the machine can show the same level of intelligence as humans. During the test, the tester enters text through the computer keyboard and outputs text through the screen.

    1950

  • The concept of AI

    The concept of artificial intelligence was first introduced by John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky and other scientists at Dartmouth College in the USA in August 1956. After the concept was introduced, a number of remarkable research results were achieved.

    1956

  • The world's first chatbot

    The ELIZA effect is named after the ELIZA chat robot, which intentionally compares the chat robot's answering behavior to a real person's dialogue.The robot was developed by Joseph Weisenbaum at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.Simulates a Rogerian psychotherapist answering textual statements or questions posed by the user.

    1966

  • Deep Blue defeats Kasparov

    On May 11, 1997, IBM Deep Blue finally defeated chess master Kasparov 3.5:2.5 to become the winner of the New York Chess Human-Machine Tournament with a total prize of 1.1 million US dollars, and became The first computer to beat a world champion in chess.

    1997

  • Google self-driving car

    On October 9, 2010, Google officially announced its self-driving car plan. The project was initiated by Sebastian Thrun, former head of the Stanford Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (SAIL), and Anthony Levandowski, founder of 510 Systems and Anthony Robotics.

    2010

  • Implementation of ChatGTP

    ChatGPT is a natural language processing tool driven by artificial intelligence technology. It can conduct conversations by understanding and learning human language, and can even complete tasks such as writing emails, video scripts, copywriting, translation, code, and writing papers.

    2022
